Guidelines on Acceptable Language

POPVOX is a soapbox to quantify and amplify your voice on matters before the U.S. Congress. That said, it is not a forum for general political discussion and we encourage the use of a civil tone. While we hope we will not have to interfere with comments posted on the website, the following is a summary of our policy on acceptable language:

Your comments here must pertain to legislation and must:

  • not be profane, harassing, or threatening to others
  • not create a risk of harm to anyone
  • not include the personal, private information of others
  • not contain information that would be illegal for you to distribute
  • not otherwise violate the rights of others

If a comment does not satisfy these standards, we will remove the comment from the legislative reports that other users see, and we will notify the commenter of our decision. The comment will continue to be counted toward the aggregate public sentiment statistics on POPVOX, and we will deliver the message to Congress provided it would not be unlawful for us to do so. If the user chooses to revise the comment so that it satisfies these standards, then the comment will be displayed. POPVOX reserves the right to decide, in its sole discretion, whether or not a comment satisfies the standards.

Note that this is a summary of our content terms. Our Terms of Use Agreement sets out the standards in full.
