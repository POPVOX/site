{% more intro %}

Creating laws is the most important responsibility of Congress. Each proposed law begins as legislation, or a bill, sponsored by a Member of Congress in the House or the Senate. Once a bill is introduced, it is shepherded through various committees before it comes to the House or Senate floor for a vote. Once both the House and Senate pass the bill, it goes to the President to be signed into law.

Advocacy organizations and trade associations play a significant role in this process. They can help push bills through the process by lobbying Members of Congress to co-sponsor the bill and garnering public support for the bill to persuade Congress — or even specific Members — to act.

Your role in the lawmaking process is just as critical: to help Congress understand your opinions. POPVOX simplifies this task by allowing you to voice your concerns about specific legislation by issue area and delivers your message directly to your Members of Congress in a format that is tailored for Congressional staffers.
